About

Driven by Dignity. Designed for Impact.

  • 2000-2010

    Learning the Landscape Through Lived Experience

    Datta Advocate was born from survival. Our founder navigated housing instability, food insecurity, and community barriers. She experienced how hard it is for families to access support when the systems meant to help are fragmented and unwelcoming. These years shaped a deep understanding of what it takes to survive and revealed how much emotional labor goes unseen when families are forced to fight for basic dignity.

  • 2015-2019

    Advocacy finds it's voice

    As a Special Needs Liaison and PTA leader, our founder created inclusive programming that reached every part of the school. She coordinated Walk In My Shoes events, ran inclusive playdates, and designed art projects that helped teachers talk about disability and difference. She supported families through emotionally charged IEP and 504 processes, maintained a resource hub, and responded to outreach from parents seeking guidance. These efforts laid the foundation for trauma informed systems that reflect the real needs of students and families.

  • 2020-2021

    Building Systems That See Us

    Through her work Virginia PTA, our founder hosted a DIVE webinar series (Diversity and Inclusion Via Education), our founder helped shift public conversations. She facilitated respectful dialogue about race, disability, and belonging in school communities that often resisted those truths. She brought lived experience into policy spaces, trained families to advocate for their children, and modeled emotionally intelligent communication rooted in care and accountability.

  • 2022 to 2023

    Deepening Systems Knowledge and Coalition Building

    Datta Advocate began with survival. Our founder navigated housing instability, food insecurity, and the special education system as a parent and advocate. Over time, she supported families through IEP and 504 processes, and helped shape policy conversations at the local and state level. She deepened her understanding of school discipline and disability rights, studying how exclusionary practices contribute to the pipeline from preschool to prison. Today, Datta Advocate works at the intersection of education and housing justice, supporting families facing eviction, homelessness, and systemic barriers.
